# Day 7 — Model & Effort Selection Strategy
## ABTalks 60-Day Claude AI Mastery Challenge

---

## What I learned today

Not all tasks are equal — and neither should your Claude model choice be. Today I built a complete framework for picking the right Claude model and effort level for every type of task.

---

## The 3-model framework

| Model | Nickname | Best for |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Haiku | The Sprinter | Quick lookups, grammar fixes, hashtags, simple Q&A |
| Sonnet | The Workhorse | Coding, content, resumes, research — 85% of daily work |
| Opus | The Deep Thinker | Hard DSA, system design, career decisions |

---

## The 4 effort levels

### Low
Casual queries, quick fact checks, grammar fixes, short brainstorms. Use when speed matters more than depth.

### Standard *(your default)*
85% of tasks — coding sessions, content drafts, research summaries, resume edits, learning support.

### High
Important outputs — internship cover letters, complex bugs, detailed study plans, content strategy documents.

### Max
Reserve for critical deliverables — final resume review before sending, hard DSA problems, pivotal project architecture decisions. Use rarely.

---

## Task → model mapping

| Task | Best Model | Best Effort | Reason |
|------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Debug code / write functions | Sonnet | Standard | Strong reasoning, fast enough for iteration |
| Hard DSA / system design | Opus | High | Needs deep reasoning over complex constraints |
| LinkedIn / ABTalks posts | Sonnet | Standard | Creative + structured — Sonnet handles tone well |
| Quick hashtags / captions | Haiku | Low | Trivial output — no need for a powerful model |
| Resume tailoring (internship) | Sonnet | High | Career-critical — worth extra reasoning depth |
| Learning a new concept | Sonnet | Standard | Explains clearly with examples |
| Research essay / report | Sonnet | High | Long-form coherence benefits from higher effort |
| Marketing copy / email draft | Sonnet | Standard | Good creative balance; iterate fast |
| Grammar / spell check | Haiku | Low | Pure mechanical task — fastest model wins |
| Career decision / strategy | Opus | High | Nuanced advice benefits from deepest model |

---

## Key insights

- **Sonnet is the default** for a daily-use student juggling coding, content, career prep, and research.
- **Haiku** saves time and limits for tasks that don't need deep reasoning.
- **Opus** is not "always better" — it's slower and overkill for 90% of daily tasks.
- **Standard effort** handles most tasks well. Max effort is for final, high-stakes outputs only.
- **Vague prompts waste powerful models** — specific context = better output at any tier.

---

## Biggest mistakes to avoid

1. Using Opus for everything — it's slow and overkill for most tasks.
2. Using Max effort every time — Standard effort handles most tasks well.
3. Vague prompts with powerful models — "Help me with my resume" wastes Opus.
4. Not iterating — Claude is best used conversationally in the same chat.
5. Treating all tasks equally — a LinkedIn caption and a system design question are not the same.

---

## Final recommendation

> If you could only pick one: **Claude Sonnet + Standard Effort**
> This covers 85% of daily needs — coding, content, research, resume work, and learning — without burning time or limits.
